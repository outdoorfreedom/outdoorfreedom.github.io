Summary

-  Trail: 5km long, 260m altitude change, medium

-  Starting point: Amden Village Parking

-  Route details: Schweizmobil 702

-  Additional details: Webcams

-  Alternative longer route: Schweizmobil 701



Beginning at the Amden parking lot, I embarked on a hike to Arvenbüel, following the Schweizmobil 702 route. For those seeking a more extended trek, an alternative is available by following route 701. Both options offer a medium level of difficulty and are marked with the familiar pink signs.

The Egg-Rör Trail serves as an all-weather path, suitable for hiking even in the summer. Opting for a clockwise direction, starting from the left, adds a touch of challenge to the ascent while providing a more gradual descent.

Throughout the trail, you'll see to breathtaking views of the Glarus Alps and will pass by the source of the Seerenbach Falls. These falls, among the highest in Switzerland, cascade in three stages below the Arvenbüel over the cliff edge of the Walensee.

Benches for resting are available only at the beginning of the trail. Additionally, be mindful that at the trail's outset, the snowshoe path intersect with ski slopes.
