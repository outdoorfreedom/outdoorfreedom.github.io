Haldi Trail





Summary

-  Trail: 7km long, 400m altitude change, medium difficulty

-  Starting point: Schattdorf Lift Station Uri

-  More details: Haldi Website

-  Route details: SchweizMobil 885



The hike kicks off from the lift station, conveniently situated for a seamless start. Ample parking, including an underground section, caters to visitors. The petite gondola operates at approximately 20-minute intervals, providing regular transport. For additional details, refer to the Haldi Website.

Choosing the left side from the lift station, I commenced the hike. Initial markers are denoted by regular pink signs, transitioning to red and blue sticks for the remaining route. The initial stretch is steep, leading through a sparse forest area. Opting for a shorter route is feasible, guided by the usual pink signs, although I opted for the longest trail.

While ascending, I passed by a charming restaurant. Benches are available at the beginning of the trail, becoming less frequent later on. A noteworthy exception is a bench in the middle of the route, offering panoramic views of the mountains and the lake – a perfect spot for the photo captured above.

The descent begins, initially featuring a somewhat steep incline that gradually lessens. The final leg lacks clear signage, but the path remains easily navigable. Notably, on December 9th, 2023, the trail had minimal snow coverage, making snowshoes unnecessary for my hike.
